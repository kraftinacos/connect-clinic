Frequently asked questions

Quick answers to common questions. Still unsure? Get in touch.

Do I need a GP referral?

No. You can self-refer for ADHD assessments and our anxiety/depression pathways. No GP referral needed to book. However, we do communicate with your GP (with your consent) and can accept referrals from GPs, school counsellors, and other health professionals.

How do virtual consults work?

All consultations are conducted via secure video call. You'll receive a link before your appointment. You can join from your phone, tablet, or computer — whatever you're comfortable with. Find a private space where you feel safe to talk. That's it.

What platform do you use for video calls?

We use secure, NZ-appropriate telehealth platforms that comply with privacy requirements. You'll receive clear instructions before your appointment. No special software downloads required — just click the link and join.

Is this covered by insurance or ACC?

Connect Clinic is a private service. Some health insurance policies may cover mental health consultations — check with your insurer. ACC may cover mental health support related to an accepted injury claim. We can provide receipts for you to submit to your insurer.

What if I'm under 18?

We see patients aged 12 and up. If you're under 18, a parent or legal guardian must provide consent for the assessment or treatment. We actively involve parents and whānau in the care pathway — you'll be part of the process, not locked out of it.

What if I need medication?

Dr Katoa is a prescribing GP. If ADHD is confirmed and medication is clinically appropriate, we can prescribe. Ongoing prescribing may be coordinated with your regular GP. For anxiety and depression, medication may be discussed as part of a broader treatment plan if indicated.

What if I'm in crisis right now?

Connect Clinic is not a crisis service. If you or someone you know is in immediate danger:

  • Call 111 for emergencies
  • Call or text 1737 to talk to a trained counsellor (24/7)
  • Go to your nearest hospital emergency department

Our structured programmes are for mild to moderate concerns and are not appropriate for acute crisis situations.

How long does an ADHD assessment take?

The assessment pathway includes 2–3 virtual sessions:

  • Session 1: 60 minutes (clinical interview)
  • Session 2: 45 minutes (collateral review)
  • Session 3: 45 minutes (feedback and plan)

You'll also complete screening questionnaires between sessions. The full process typically takes 2–3 weeks from first contact to final report.

What happens after the six-week programme ends?

You'll receive a discharge summary that includes your progress (measured by PHQ-9 or GAD-7 scores), what was covered, and a maintenance plan. With your consent, we send a copy to your GP. Some people finish and don't need further support. Others may benefit from ongoing therapy — we can discuss options and referrals if needed.

Can my GP get a copy of my records?

Yes — with your explicit consent. We believe in coordinated care. For ADHD assessments, we typically send a summary to your GP. For anxiety/depression pathways, we send a discharge summary. You control what is shared and with whom. We won't share information without your permission (unless legally required for safety reasons).

Is this culturally safe for Pacific and Māori young people?

This is why we exist. Connect Clinic is Pacific-governed, not a corporate entity adding cultural safety as an afterthought. Dr Katoa is Tongan and understands the context many Pacific and Māori young people navigate. We actively involve whānau. We don't expect you to leave your culture at the door.

What if I'm not sure which service I need?

Book a consultation and tell us what's going on. Dr Katoa will help figure out the right pathway. Sometimes it's clear (you want an ADHD assessment). Sometimes it's not (you feel off but don't know why). Both are fine. We'll work it out together.
