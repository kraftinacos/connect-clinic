Could it be ADHD?

Comprehensive adult ADHD assessment with a clear pathway to diagnosis and treatment.

ADHD affects approximately 5% of adults, but many go undiagnosed. If you've always felt like you're working harder than everyone else just to keep up, or if focus and organisation have always been a struggle, an assessment could provide the answers you've been looking for.

Our Three-Session Assessment

Session 1 90 minutes

Comprehensive Clinical Interview

We begin with a detailed conversation about your:

  • Current symptoms and challenges
  • Childhood history and development
  • Educational and work experiences
  • Medical and psychiatric history
  • Family history of ADHD or related conditions

This helps us understand your unique experience and rule out other conditions that might explain your symptoms.

Session 2 60 minutes

Objective Assessment & Questionnaires

Session two involves validated assessment tools:

  • ADHD-specific rating scales
  • Cognitive screening if indicated
  • Questionnaires for you and a family member or partner
  • Review of any school reports or previous assessments

These objective measures complement the clinical interview and provide a comprehensive picture.

Session 3 45 minutes

Diagnosis & Treatment Planning

In our final session, we discuss:

  • Assessment findings and diagnosis
  • Personalised treatment recommendations
  • Medication options if appropriate
  • Non-medication strategies and supports
  • Next steps and follow-up plan

You'll receive a written summary and, if diagnosed, a treatment plan tailored to your goals.

Who can be assessed?

We assess adults 18 and over. We cannot provide ADHD assessments for:

  • Those seeking assessment solely for academic accommodations
  • Individuals currently in acute psychiatric crisis
  • Those with active substance dependence requiring treatment first

Please note: A GP referral is not required, but we do recommend having a GP as ongoing medication management requires their involvement.
