You don't have to face depression alone

Compassionate, structured support to help you find your way back to wellness.

Depression can make even the simplest tasks feel impossible. It can drain your energy, rob you of joy, and make the future feel hopeless. But with the right support, recovery is possible. Our structured programme meets you where you are and walks with you toward feeling better.

Your Six-Week Pathway

Week 1

Understanding Your Depression

We begin by understanding your experience:

  • Your unique symptoms and their impact
  • Contributing factors and triggers
  • How depression affects your thinking, body, and behaviour
  • Identifying your strengths and resources

Takeaway: A clear picture of your depression and immediate coping strategies.

Week 2

Behavioural Activation

Depression thrives on isolation and inactivity. This week we focus on:

  • The depression-activity cycle
  • Identifying values and meaningful activities
  • Starting small and building momentum
  • Scheduling pleasant and mastery activities

Takeaway: A personalised activity plan to start shifting the depression cycle.

Week 3

Working with Difficult Thoughts

Depression often comes with harsh, critical thoughts:

  • Recognising depression's voice vs reality
  • Self-compassion practices
  • Challenging hopeless predictions
  • Building more balanced self-talk

Takeaway: Tools to relate differently to depressive thinking.

Week 4

Connection & Relationships

Depression often isolates us from others:

  • Understanding social withdrawal
  • Rebuilding connections gradually
  • Communicating your needs to others
  • Seeking and accepting support

Takeaway: A plan for reconnecting with others at your own pace.

Week 5

Sleep, Body & Routine

Taking care of the basics supports recovery:

  • Sleep hygiene and depression
  • Gentle movement and its benefits
  • Nutrition and mood
  • Building sustainable routines

Takeaway: Practical lifestyle changes that support mental health.

Week 6

Relapse Prevention & Moving Forward

Planning for lasting wellness:

  • Recognising early warning signs
  • What to do if symptoms return
  • Consolidating what's helped
  • Building a long-term wellness plan

Takeaway: A written relapse prevention plan and clear next steps.

Who this is for

  • Adults experiencing depression of mild to moderate severity
  • Those feeling stuck, hopeless, or persistently low
  • People ready to make changes, even when it feels hard
  • Anyone wanting structured, compassionate support

Who this is not for

  • Those experiencing severe depression requiring hospital care
  • Anyone with active suicidal intent (please contact Crisis services immediately)
  • People with active substance dependence
  • Those with untreated bipolar disorder (we recommend mood stabilisation first)

Investment

$150 per session ($900 total for six weeks). Sessions are 50 minutes via secure video call.

If you're in crisis

This programme is not a crisis service. If you're having thoughts of harming yourself, please contact:

  • 1737 - Free call or text any time for support from a trained counsellor
  • Emergency Services: 111 - If you or someone else is in immediate danger
  • Your local DHB Crisis Team
