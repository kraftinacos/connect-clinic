Anxiety doesn't have to run your life

A structured, six-week pathway to understanding and managing your anxiety.

Anxiety is one of the most common mental health challenges, affecting 1 in 4 New Zealanders. The good news? It's highly treatable. Our six-week programme gives you the tools, understanding, and support to take back control.

Your Six-Week Pathway

Week 1

Understanding Your Anxiety

We start by mapping your anxiety:

  • Your unique triggers and patterns
  • How anxiety shows up in your body
  • The thoughts that keep it going
  • Your personal anxiety story

Takeaway: A personalised anxiety profile and immediate grounding techniques.

Week 2

The Body Connection

Anxiety lives in the body as much as the mind. This week we explore:

  • The nervous system and anxiety
  • Breathing techniques that actually work
  • Progressive muscle relaxation
  • Building your calm-down toolkit

Takeaway: A set of physical calming strategies tailored to you.

Week 3

Thought Patterns

Learning to work with anxious thoughts:

  • Identifying thinking traps
  • Cognitive restructuring techniques
  • Defusion from anxious thoughts
  • Building balanced thinking habits

Takeaway: Skills to challenge and reframe anxious thinking.

Week 4

Facing Fears

Gradual, supported exposure to anxiety triggers:

  • Understanding avoidance cycles
  • Creating an exposure hierarchy
  • Practising approach over avoidance
  • Building confidence through action

Takeaway: A personalised plan for gradually facing feared situations.

Week 5

Lifestyle & Prevention

Setting yourself up for long-term wellness:

  • Sleep and anxiety
  • Exercise and movement
  • Substances and anxiety
  • Building supportive routines

Takeaway: Lifestyle adjustments to support your mental health.

Week 6

Your Maintenance Plan

Consolidating your progress:

  • Reviewing what's worked
  • Identifying your early warning signs
  • Creating your relapse prevention plan
  • Planning ongoing support if needed

Takeaway: A written maintenance plan and clear next steps.

Who this is for

  • Adults experiencing generalised anxiety, social anxiety, or panic
  • Those whose anxiety is affecting work, relationships, or daily life
  • People ready to commit to weekly sessions and between-session practice
  • Anyone wanting structured, evidence-based support

Who this is not for

  • Those in acute crisis requiring immediate hospital-level care
  • People with severe, untreated trauma (we recommend trauma-focused therapy first)
  • Individuals with active substance dependence
  • Those unable to commit to weekly attendance
