About Connect Clinic

Pacific-led mental health care for Aotearoa

Connect Clinic was founded with a clear vision: to make quality mental health care more accessible to all New Zealanders. As Pacific clinicians, we bring cultural understanding and clinical excellence together to provide care that truly connects.

Our Clinicians

FK

Dr Francis Katoa

Psychiatrist & Clinic Director

Raised in a traditional Tongan household, Dr Francis Katoa is a Fellow of the Royal Australian and New Zealand College of Psychiatrists with specialist expertise in adult ADHD, mood disorders, and anxiety conditions.

With over a decade of experience in both public and private mental health services across Aotearoa, Dr Katoa has developed a reputation for thorough, compassionate care. He is particularly passionate about improving access to ADHD assessment for adults who may have gone undiagnosed.

Dr Katoa is of Tongan descent and is committed to providing culturally safe care for Pacific peoples, while welcoming patients from all backgrounds.

Dr Staverton Kautoke

Psychiatrist

Dr Staverton Kautoke is a Fellow of the Royal Australian and New Zealand College of Psychiatrists with particular interests in anxiety disorders, depression, and the mental health of young adults.

Dr Kautoke brings extensive experience from his work in community mental health and specialist anxiety services. He is known for his warm, collaborative approach and his skill in combining medication management with psychological strategies.

Of Tongan heritage, Dr Kautoke is dedicated to reducing stigma around mental health in Pacific communities and ensuring care is delivered in a culturally responsive manner.

Governance

Connect Clinic operates with transparency and accountability. We are committed to:

Clinical Governance

All our clinicians are registered with the Medical Council of New Zealand and participate in ongoing professional development. We maintain robust clinical records and adhere to the highest standards of psychiatric care.

Privacy & Confidentiality

Your privacy is paramount. We operate in accordance with the Privacy Act 2020 and Health Information Privacy Code. All consultations are conducted via secure, encrypted platforms.

Cultural Safety

We are committed to providing culturally safe care for Māori, Pacific peoples, and all communities in Aotearoa. We acknowledge the principles of Te Tiriti o Waitangi and strive to reduce health inequities.

Quality Improvement

We regularly review our services and welcome feedback. Patient outcomes and satisfaction guide our continuous improvement efforts.
