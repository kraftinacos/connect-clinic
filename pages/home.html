Who is this for?

"I think I might have ADHD but I've never been assessed"

You're not alone. Many young people reach their 20s realising ADHD might explain years of struggles with focus, organisation, and motivation. Our structured assessment gives you clarity — not just a label, but a real understanding of what's going on and what to do about it.

Learn about ADHD assessment →
"I've been feeling anxious and it's affecting my study/work"

Anxiety shows up differently for everyone — racing thoughts, physical tension, avoiding things you need to do. Our six-week anxiety pathway gives you practical tools that actually work, not just talking. And we measure your progress so you can see the change.

Learn about anxiety support →
"I've been feeling low and disconnected for weeks"

Feeling flat, unmotivated, or disconnected isn't weakness — it's a signal that something needs attention. Our depression pathway helps you understand what's happening and rebuild momentum through practical, structured support.

Learn about depression support →
"I'm a parent/whānau member worried about my young person"

We actively involve parents and whānau in the care pathway for under-18s. You know your young person best. We bring clinical expertise. Together we build a plan that works. For ADHD assessments, we gather collateral information from parents as part of the diagnostic process.

"I'm a GP looking to refer a patient for structured mental health support"

Connect Clinic sits in the gap between brief GP consults and secondary mental health services. We provide structured, outcome-measured interventions with clear discharge summaries back to you. Our pathways are evidence-based, time-limited, and designed to return patients to your care with improved function.
