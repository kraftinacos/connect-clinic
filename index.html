The Missing Middle

Too unwell for a GP. Not unwell enough for a specialist.

"Mental health services and support currently available to young people is fragmented, and not all young people have timely and barrier-free access to appropriate mental health care."

‚Äî Auditor-General of New Zealand, 2024

I've worked in the clinics. I've seen how the system fails. A young person presents to their GP with anxiety or depression. The GP has 15 minutes and a prescription pad. There's no structured pathway, no outcome tracking, no follow-up that actually works.

Specialist services have 8-week waitlists and strict thresholds. Private therapy costs \$150‚Äì200 a session. And the Access and Choice programme ‚Äî despite \$455 million in funding ‚Äî still doesn't reach most of the people who need it.

This is the missing middle. Too complex for a 15-minute GP consult. Not in crisis enough for a CMHT. Too broke for a psychologist. Too disengaged for a system that expects them to show up, sit still, and fit the criteria.

Crisis / Specialist Services

CAMHS, CMHTs, acute inpatient. High threshold. Long waitlists. You need to be very unwell to qualify.

The Missing Middle

Mild to moderate anxiety, depression, stress, ADHD, grief, sleep. Too complex for 15 min with a GP. Not severe enough for secondary care. This is where most young people sit ‚Äî and where the system has the biggest gap.

General Practice / Primary Care

15-minute consults. Limited mental health training. No structured pathways. No outcome measurement. Prescriptions and referrals, mostly.

About

Built by a clinician who's been in the room

Connect Clinic is led by Dr Francis Katoa, a New Zealand GP and RNZCGP Fellow with seven years in high-needs primary care. Trained in CBT, youth mental health, and medicinal cannabis prescribing.

This isn't a startup that read a report and built an app. This is a clinician who has sat across from young people in under-resourced clinics, watched them fall through the cracks, and decided to build what the system won't.

Connect operates under Pasifika Rise Limited. Pacific-governed, Aotearoa-based, and accountable to the communities it serves.
